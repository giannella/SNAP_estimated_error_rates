Background and Definitions

Congress is considering removing the SNAP QC tolerance threshold, which excludes small errors (currently $58 or less) from a state's payment error rate (PER). Beginning in FY28, states with error rates exceeding 6%, 8%, and 10% will need to cover 5%, 10%, and 15% of benefits issued, respectively. These analyses use the most recent available data (FY23 and FY24) to project the potential impact of removing the tolerance threshold in terms of those cost shares. Base rates are from FY24, which reflect official error numbers, and the estimated change due to removing the threshold is computed using public QC data (FY23). See sources below.
